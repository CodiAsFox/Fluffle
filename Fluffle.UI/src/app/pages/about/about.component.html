Fluffle is an initiative taken by me, Noppes, with the goal to eventually create a single unified platform for fellow furries with which they can reverse search artwork from a variety of sources. However, at the moment Fluffle only has support for e621 due to them providing an easy way for developers to integrate with their service.

In its early stages Fluffle will remain closed source, but I might decide to make it public domain under the GPLv3 (The GNU General Public License v3.0) license in the future.

Privacy

Fluffle has been created with privacy in mind from the start. Due to Fluffle its scope being limited – after all, you can only reverse search images – we can ensure Fluffle respects your privacy.

When you reverse search an image on Fluffle, the image gets stored temporarily on our servers for processing. Processing only contains fingerprinting the image. That is creating an identifier which can be used to compare indexed images. Once the fingerprint is created, the submitted image gets permanently deleted immediately. The aforementioned fingerprint also doesn’t get stored and neither do the settings used for reverse searching.

Said settings, from here on called preferences, are stored in your browser its so-called local storage. You can think of this as a small place in which websites can store a small amount of data. These preferences are only sent to Fluffle whenever you submit an image for reverse searching, and as said before, are immediately discarded when they are not required anymore for fulfilling your request.

Cookies

The only cookies Fluffle makes use of, are those required for the services provided by Cloudflare. At this moment, Cloudflare only requires the use of the __cfduid cookie. Despite its misleading name, it is only used by Cloudflare for detecting malicious visitors. So, you are not being tracked in any way. In fact, from the May 10 they are going to stop using it altogether.

If you would like to learn more about Cloudflare its cookie usage, we kindly refer you to Cloudflare its website where they are thoroughly explained.
